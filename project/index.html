Blue tits (Cyanistes caeruleus) have been shown to adjust their awakening time according to local light conditions. Hence, light pollution may cause animals to wake up earlier and potentially sleep less or, as cessation of activity can be delayed, also fall asleep later. In contrast to laboratory studies, whether and how artificial light affects sleep behaviour in free-living birds has not yet been studied.
